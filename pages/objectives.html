What we set out to do.

A vision needs footholds. We pursue ours through three clear objectives — turning the idea of a mountain at peace into the daily, deliberate work of awareness, livelihood, and justice.

Our objectives

Three commitments that guide every step.

Each objective answers a different question the Himalaya keeps asking — what people know, how they live, and whether the gains are shared.

Mission 01

Awareness that holds

Develop awareness among the general population, stakeholders, and policymakers about protecting the physical and biological components of the environment — so that care for the mountain becomes common ground, not a specialist's concern.

Mission 02

Development without disturbance

Create location-specific developmental models that secure health and livelihood without disturbing the environment — built with every stakeholder, fitted to the terrain and the people who call it home.

Mission 03

Equity that endures

Establish strategies for equitable living standards — social and economic justice, gender equity, and the sustainable sharing of natural resources — because conservation only lasts when its benefits reach everyone.

Where we focus

Guarding a fragile, living landscape.

Our objectives meet the ground in one of the planet's most biodiverse and delicate regions: the Darjeeling Hills and the wider Kanchenjunga landscape. Here, a single slope can hold species found nowhere else on Earth.

Biodiversity conservation

We work to keep the hill ecosystems intact — the forests, watercourses, and soils that hold the mountain together and let its life continue to evolve on its own terms.

Protecting endemic species

From globally threatened plants and animals to insects and avifauna, we focus on the species that depend entirely on this landscape. Protect their habitat, and you protect the whole.

  • Fragile Darjeeling Hill ecosystems and the Kanchenjunga landscape.
  • Globally threatened endemic plants, animals, insects, and birds.
  • The communities whose livelihoods are woven into both.
